## What this sprint tested
The Phase-0 conclusion named two levers to push past the ~0.65 family-accuracy ceiling WITHOUT
more data: (#1) a stronger KG-vs-RAG family signal, (#2) VAL-calibrated abstention. We built three
offline arms and measured them on the 97 blind-gold rows (entity-disjoint = the primary honesty split,
422 train / 97 gold). The anti-fab gate (no new `false_honest_partial` / `wrong_confident_exact` vs
`detector_first`) was the hard constraint.

## Per-lever verdict
1. **Lever #1a — class-balance (`max_per_label=100`): NO EFFECT.** Identical to `masked_coarse` on
   every gold metric. Max-sim-per-label voting is already largely count-invariant, and slot-masking
   already removed the dominant-token bias, so the modest KG(188)/RAG(207) imbalance was not driving
   misroutes. The knob stays in the harness; it is not part of the shipped config.
2. **Lever #1b — KG-recall bias (inverse-FN analogue): REJECT.** It recovered one KG-vs-RAG drop
   (struct-FN 18→17) but converted that question into a **wrong-skill** dispatch (wce 1→2, skill
   0.952→0.909). Trading a safe RAG fallback for a wrong-confident-exact is exactly the hazard the gate
   guards — net-negative vs the floor even though it stays ≤ the baseline. The PRODUCTION inverse-FN
   guard (spec §5 step 4) is still worth building, but it must gate on the resolver actually validating
   the slots, not merely on `route()` returning non-None; this arm shows the naive version backfires.
3. **Lever #2 — VAL-calibrated abstention: MECHANISM FIXED, OPERATING POINT WRONG.** The Phase-0 inert
   bug is gone — abstention is now ACTIVE (VAL-calibrated margin 0.0783, target precision 0.9). But at
   that margin it OVER-abstains: family accuracy craters 0.660→0.454 and struct-FN balloons 18→24 as
   KG/OTHER questions get dumped to CLARIFY. Do NOT enable at this operating point. The fix is a
   coverage-floored / per-family recalibration (cap abstention's coverage cost, or calibrate per family
   so it only fires on the genuinely ambiguous tail) — a Phase-1b lever, not a Phase-1a defect.

## The core finding (quantified)
The family-accuracy headroom is real but **only the anti-fab-LEAKY arms capture it**: `full_classifier`
/ `masked_full` reach 0.78–0.80 family and 4–7 struct-FN, but at 6–7 `wrong_confident_exact` → gate-
rejected. The anti-fab-SAFE coarse arms sit at ~0.66 family / ~18 struct-FN. **None of the three offline
levers moved the safe arm off that floor.** The ~0.65 ceiling for a leak-free router is therefore not
crackable by class-balance, a naive inverse-FN bias, or abstention-as-configured. The genuine path to
higher leak-free family accuracy is a better KG-vs-RAG *signal* (and/or the deferred Phase-2 LLM
slot-recovery on the KG-classified-but-unresolved residue) — that is now Phase 1b's central research
question, with these three knobs ruled out as the answer.

## Go/No-Go decision
**GO to Phase 1b — ship `masked_coarse` (plain masked coarse-family classifier → deterministic skill
selection).** It beats `detector_first` on family (0.660 vs 0.577), skill (0.952 vs 0.909), and anti-fab
(wce 1 vs 2) while gate-clean — the design's floor guarantee. **No lever is adopted into the shipped
config.** Abstention stays BUILT but OFF (mechanism validated, needs recalibration in 1b). The inverse-FN
guard is built in production per spec §5 but with the resolver-validation precondition above.
