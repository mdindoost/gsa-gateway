## GSA Constitution — Overview
This document contains key provisions of the NJIT Graduate Student Association Constitution. The full constitution is available at the GSA office (Campus Center 110A). All graduate students currently enrolled at NJIT are automatic members of the GSA with full voting rights.

## Officer Eligibility Requirements
To run for any GSA Executive Board position (President, VP Academic Affairs, VP Finance, VP Social, Secretary, or Treasurer), a candidate must meet ALL of the following requirements:
- Be a currently enrolled NJIT graduate student in good academic standing
- Have a minimum cumulative GPA of 3.00 at the time of nomination
- Have been enrolled at NJIT for at least 1 full academic year (two semesters) prior to the election
- Have attended at least 3 GSA general meetings in the preceding semester
- Not be on academic probation or under any active disciplinary proceedings
- Submit a completed nomination form by the published deadline

Failure to meet any single requirement disqualifies the candidate from the election.

## Presidential Requirements
In addition to the standard officer eligibility requirements above, candidates for GSA President must:
- Have a minimum cumulative GPA of 3.00
- Have been enrolled at NJIT for at least 1 full academic year (two semesters)
- Have served in at least one GSA leadership role (officer, committee chair, or club president) for at least one semester
- Submit a 500-word platform statement with their nomination form
- Obtain signatures from at least 10 current GSA members on the nomination petition

## Election Process
Elections are held each April. The nomination period opens March 1 and closes March 15. Candidates must present a 3-minute speech at the April general meeting. Voting is conducted electronically via NJIT student email over a 72-hour window. Results are announced within 48 hours of voting closing. A runoff election is held if no candidate receives more than 50% of valid votes cast.

## Quorum Requirements
A quorum for general meetings requires the presence of at least 15 GSA members, or 10% of the total membership, whichever is greater. Executive Board meetings require at least 4 of the 7 board members to be present. A quorum is required to pass any official resolution, to amend the constitution, or to allocate funds exceeding $1,000. If quorum is not reached, the meeting may proceed informally but no binding votes may be taken.

## Constitutional Amendments
Amendments to the GSA Constitution require a two-thirds majority vote of members present at a quorate general meeting. Proposed amendments must be submitted in writing to the Secretary at least 2 weeks before the vote. All members must be notified of proposed amendments via email at least 1 week in advance. Approved amendments take effect at the start of the following academic semester.

## Membership Rights
All currently enrolled NJIT graduate students are members of the GSA with the right to: attend general meetings, vote in GSA elections, run for office if eligible, access GSA resources and funding programs, and submit initiatives for board consideration. Membership is free and automatic — no registration required. Membership is suspended upon disenrollment from NJIT.

## Removal from Office
An officer may be removed from office by a two-thirds vote of the Executive Board, provided the officer has been notified in writing at least 1 week in advance and given an opportunity to address the board. Grounds for removal include: academic probation, three unexcused absences from board meetings in one semester, or a finding of misconduct by NJIT Student Affairs.
