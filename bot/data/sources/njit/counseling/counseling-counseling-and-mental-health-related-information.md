Counseling and Mental Health Related Information
They are not intended to substitute for the process of personal counseling. You may find each of the following sites thought provoking, interesting, and hopefully helpful.
Please be advised however, that the NJIT Center for Counseling and Psychological Services (C-CAPS) is not responsible for the content of these sites and can not guarantee the accuracy or quality of the information that they contain.

Links to Counseling and Mental Health Related Information
Go Ask Alice!
-- Is the health question and answer site produced by Columbia University's Health Education Program. It answers questions about relationships, sexuality, emotional health, fitness, alcohol, nicotine, and other drugs, as well as general health questions.
Psychology In Daily Life
is a site produced and maintained by the American Psychological Association. It provides information on a variety of topics, such as stress management, anger management, sexual orientation, and depression.
Campus Blues
provides articles, coping strategies and other information about numerous issues that affect college students, ranging from depression, eating disorders or substance abuse to test anxiety, coming out, assertiveness, loneliness, or roommate relationships.
https://researchguides.njit.edu/self-help
The NJIT library provides numerous self-help books recommended by Center for Counseling and Psychological Services (C-CAPS.) The topics reflect the problems and conditions that the counseling staff observe in their own student clients. The books listed are available at the library for check out at your convenience. They are located on the first floor of the library.
