Group Therapy and Support Groups
Spring 2026 Support Groups and Weekly Therapy Groups at C-CAPS
Therapy groups and drop-in support groups are confidential spaces that offer the unique experience of gaining support  with a variety of concerns, practicing communication, learning new skills and strategies, and building social connections.  Led by the licensed mental health professionals at C-CAPS, groups can be attended in addition to or in lieu of individual  counseling or therapy at C-CAPS when appropriate. Take a moment to review this semester’s offerings.
Our Services
Group Therapy Offerings - Spring 2026 Drop-In Support Groups (Registration is not required)
Let’s Talk Drop-In Group - Mondays: 4:00 - 5:15 pm
Facilitated by Dr. Phyllis Bolling
This group provides an opportunity to discuss concerns and challenges you may be experiencing, get support, discuss ways of coping and gain a greater understanding of yourself and others. Some of the concerns may include: anxiety, academic pressures, difficult or unsatisfactory relationships (friends, family or romantic, classmates etc.), isolation, depression, loss, or self-esteem. Location: Campus Center, Room 290 (2nd Floor)
Men’s Corner Drop-In Group - Tuesdays: 4:00 - 5:15 pm
Facilitated by Mr. Will Zarillo
This is a men’s group focused on mental health, balance, and connection. This group offers a space for men to slow down, reflect, and talk honestly about the pressures they are navigating—academically, socially, 1 | Page emotionally, and personally. Each session includes a guiding theme, brief psychoeducation, and facilitated group discussion shaped by the needs and interests of those present. Topics may include stress and the body, sleep and burnout, relationships, anxiety, isolation, and building meaningful connections. While themes are offered across the series, participants are welcome to attend one or multiple sessions and may join at any point. Location: Campus Center, Room 290 (2nd Floor)

Facilitated by Ms. Maham Tariq
This group provides a supportive and interactive space for students looking to enhance their ability to manage and navigate all types of relationships. The goal is to foster healthier, more fulfilling connections by equipping students with the tools they need to effectively navigate and understand relationship dynamics.  Location: Please call C-CAPS at 973-596-3414 for more information.
