# Full-Time Enrollment and Full-Time Certification (International Students)

All F-1 international students must be enrolled full-time every Fall and Spring semester. Summer and winter term enrollment is optional unless it is your final (graduating) semester. Dropping below full-time enrollment without prior authorization from the Office of Global Initiatives (OGI) is a serious violation of your F-1 status and can cause termination of your SEVIS record.

NJIT courses use six modes of instruction. "Face-to-face," "converged learning," and "hybrid" are considered in-person, and F-1 and J-1 international students must attend all converged-learning classes in person. All other modes are considered online. You must have at least one in-person class in your final semester, even if that semester is a summer term.

## Undergraduate students

Undergraduate students must enroll in 12 credits every Spring and Fall semester. Summer enrollment is optional. Only 3 online credits per semester count toward full-time enrollment, so you must have at least 9 in-person credits each semester.

You may drop below 12 credits only if you are in your last semester and do not need 12 credits to graduate, or you have medical or academic reasons that warrant it. In your final semester you must apply for Full-Time Certification (FTC) to avoid SEVIS termination. For a reduced course load you must submit the Adviser Form for Reduced Course Load or the Reduced Course Load for Medical Reasons Request Form; medical documentation from a U.S.-based doctor is required for a medical reduced course load. You are still limited to 3 online credits, must keep at least one in-person class, and must have prior OGI authorization to drop below full-time.

## Graduate students

Graduate students must enroll in 9 credits every Spring and Fall semester. Summer enrollment is optional. Only 3 online credits per semester count toward full-time enrollment, so you must have at least 6 in-person credits each semester.

You may drop below 9 credits only if you are in your last semester and need fewer than 9 credits to graduate, you are taking fewer credits due to CPT authorization for a co-op or internship, or you have medical reasons. In the last-semester and CPT cases you must apply for Full-Time Certification (FTC) to avoid SEVIS termination. For a medical reduced load, doctoral students request Full-Time Certification online through the Highlander Pipeline, while Master's students submit the Reduced Course Load for Medical Reasons Request Form (U.S.-based medical documentation required). All students under 9 credits are still limited to 3 online credits and must keep at least one in-person class.

For example, Master's students typically follow one of these credit sequences. Option 1 is 9-9-9-3, graduating at the end of the fourth semester. Option 2 is 12-12-6, graduating at the end of the third semester. Master's thesis students may be Full-Time Certified twice when enrolled in only 3 thesis credits; students cannot take 6 thesis credits at once, so thesis credits must be combined with other courses.

## Ph.D. students

Ph.D. students may have multiple semesters of below-full-time enrollment due to dissertation work only. You may drop below 9 credits if you have passed the qualifying exams and are required to enroll in only one dissertation credit; you must still apply for Full-Time Certification (FTC) to avoid SEVIS termination. Only 3 online credits per semester count toward full-time enrollment, so you must have at least 6 in-person credits each semester.

To request Full-Time Certification, log into the Highlander Pipeline, go to International Student SVCS, and select Full-Time Certification (FTC). Complete the FTC form only after you have finalized your schedule for the semester; if you change your schedule afterward, you must submit it again. The OGI reviews all FTC submissions once the semester begins.
