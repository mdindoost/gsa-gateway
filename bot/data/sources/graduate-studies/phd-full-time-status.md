# Full-Time Status of Ph.D. Students

For academic purposes, at least nine credits (not counting any audit credits) of course registration in the Fall and Spring semesters are required to maintain full-time (FT) status. However, students may be certified full-time with fewer than nine credits in the following cases. The minimum number of credits is shown in each case, since the academic program may have additional requirements.

Students who enrolled for the first time in the Ph.D. program after August 2015 may be certified full-time as follows:

- With three credits of pre-doctoral research, if they have completed all required courses and passed the qualifying exam.
- With three credits in the second, third, or fourth semester, if they have not passed the qualifying exam but have completed the required courses with a cumulative GPA of 3.5 or higher.
- With six credits (including three credits of pre-doctoral research, with Graduate Studies Office approval), if they have completed all required courses, have not passed the qualifying exam, and have a cumulative GPA below 3.5.
- With one credit of dissertation research, if they have completed all required courses, passed the qualifying exam, and successfully defended the dissertation proposal.

PhD students registered for fewer than nine credits who are either domestic students receiving financial support or international students (F1/J1) must apply online for full-time certification for that semester. A course registration hold is automatically placed after approval, and any further course registration changes require permission from the Graduate Studies Office.
