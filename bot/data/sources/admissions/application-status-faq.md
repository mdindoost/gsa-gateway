# Graduate Application Status and Common Questions

## Checking your application status
Check your status online through the application portal at **connect.njit.edu/apply**. Your application is reviewed once **all required documents are received** by the Admissions Office. Admission decisions are sent **directly to you by email and in the application portal** — they cannot be given over the phone.

## Documents
Unofficial copies of required documents are accepted to complete your application; admitted students must submit official documents before enrolling. A friend may inquire about missing documents, but the decision goes only to the applicant.

## Applying and changing programs
- You may apply to **one degree program at a time**.
- To **change programs**, you may apply for a department transfer **after completing one full year** in your enrolled program (a formal application and fee are required; the new department reviews it).
- You may take up to **9 credits of non-matriculated coursework** before admission with written department approval — **except F-1/J-1 international students**, who may not register as non-matriculated.

## Financial aid
All applicants are **automatically considered** for departmental financial support at the time of admission — there is no separate form. Limited graduate assistantships are available through the Office of Graduate Studies.

## International students (I-20)
Your I-20 (visa eligibility form) is issued **after you are formally admitted and your financial documentation is approved**, and it is e-mailed to you. See the OGI international guides for visa details.

Questions: Office of University Admissions, admissions@njit.edu.
