# Test Scores and English Proficiency for Graduate Admission

## GRE / GMAT — required or optional?
Whether the GRE is required depends on your college and degree level:
- **Newark College of Engineering** — GRE optional but strongly recommended for MS and PhD.
- **Ying Wu College of Computing** — GRE optional but strongly recommended for MS; **required for all PhD applicants**.
- **Jordan Hu College of Science and Liberal Arts** — GRE optional for MS and PhD.
- **Hillier College of Architecture and Design** — GRE optional for master's; **required for PhD**.
- **Martin Tuchman School of Management** — GMAT/GRE may be **waived for master's applicants with a cumulative undergraduate GPA of 3.0** (international students need a WES or other NACES-approved evaluation); required for all PhD.
- **Online programs** — GRE/GMAT optional.

Some Professional Science Master's programs (applied physics, architecture, biology, infrastructure planning, materials science) require the GRE, and applicants whose most recent degree was earned outside the U.S. must submit GRE scores. Individual academic departments set the required GRE/GMAT scores.

## English proficiency (international applicants)
Required for international applicants who do **not** hold a degree from a U.S. institution. Minimum scores:
- **TOEFL**: 79 internet-based (4.0 on the new scale); 213 computer-based; 550 paper-based
- **IELTS**: 6.5 overall, with no sub-score below 6.0
- **Duolingo**: 120
- **PTE**: 57

Submit official scores through the testing service. Questions: Office of University Admissions, admissions@njit.edu.
