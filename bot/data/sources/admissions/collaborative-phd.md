# Collaborative Ph.D. Program

The Collaborative Ph.D. lets working professionals pursue a Ph.D. part-time while employed full-time, drawing on both NJIT's and their employer's resources. Admission and academic requirements are the same as NJIT's regular Ph.D. programs; applicants must have worked in their field for at least one year, and the main dissertation advisor is an NJIT faculty member.

There are two tracks:
- **Traditional track** — you use your employer's facilities (e.g., lab or computing infrastructure) for dissertation research, and/or your employer suggests an in-house researcher with a terminal degree to co-advise or join your dissertation committee.
- **Employer-Sponsored track** — requires a major employer commitment and can lead to a joint Intellectual Property (IP) agreement with NJIT. The Graduate Studies Office runs a pre-qualification assessment (verification of full-time employment, a work portfolio, a three-page statement of purpose, transcripts, and written employer commitments including an assigned Ph.D.-holding mentor).

Anyone interested should first contact the **Vice Provost for Graduate Studies** to discuss requirements and possible paths to the degree. More information: Office of Graduate Studies.
