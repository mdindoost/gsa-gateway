# On-Campus Employment for F-1 Students

F-1 students may work in **on-campus** positions at NJIT while enrolled. "On-campus employment" means NJIT campus jobs only, unless specially authorized. International students should apply for **Institutional Work-Study (IWS)** — F-1 students are **not** eligible for federal work-study. On-campus employment does not require CPT or OPT authorization.

## Hours and eligibility rules
- Maintain your F-1 status by being enrolled full-time during the required semesters (fall and spring).
- Work **up to 20 hours per week while classes are in session** (fall and spring); you may work more than 20 hours during scheduled breaks (winter or summer).
- Work only within your program start and end dates — you **cannot work on campus past the end date on your I-20** (which is shortened to the last day of classes in your graduating semester).
- Organizations that operate within the university (such as dining services) count as on-campus. **NJII (New Jersey Innovation Institute) and VentureLink are not on-campus employers.**
- You may work for multiple offices on campus as long as your total weekly hours stay within the limits above.

## How to apply
1. Log in to **Highlander Pipeline** with your NJIT UCID → Student Services tab → Other Resources → **On-Campus Employment**.
2. Create an online application, then search postings, apply, and check your status.
3. Once hired, your employment paperwork must be processed and you must receive clearance to work from **Student Financial Aid Services (SFAS)** before you begin.

You will need a **Social Security Number** to finalize employment: SFAS issues a confirmation letter and forwards it to OGI for your adviser's signature, after which you apply at the Social Security Administration.

## Confirm with OGI
For questions about on-campus work and F-1 status, contact OGI: https://www.njit.edu/global
