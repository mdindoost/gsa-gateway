# Optional Practical Training (OPT) and STEM OPT

Always use the current Form I-765, filing fee, and mailing address from the USCIS website (linked on the OGI page). USCIS updates these, and it is your responsibility to follow the current requirements to avoid a rejection or denial.

## What it is
Optional Practical Training (OPT) is USCIS work authorization for F-1 students to work in a job directly related to your major. It requires USCIS approval before you start working. You may qualify for up to 12 months of full-time OPT for each higher level of education you complete.
- **Pre-completion OPT** is before you finish your program; most NJIT students use CPT instead, and any pre-completion OPT time is deducted from your 12 months.
- **Post-completion OPT** is after program completion, and is what most students apply for.

## Eligibility
- Be in F-1 status and currently enrolled, or within 60 days of your program end date.
- Have completed at least one full academic year of full-time enrollment, on campus in the U.S.
- Complete the OPT lesson and quiz in the OGI Canvas course (the certificate is required with your application).
- Meet NJIT's graduation GPA (2.0 undergraduate / 3.0 graduate). This is an NJIT graduation policy, not a USCIS rule.

## When to apply (post-completion OPT — strict deadlines)
USCIS is very strict; missing a deadline by even one day usually means denial. Your application must be **received by USCIS**:
- no earlier than **90 days before** your program end date,
- no later than **60 days after** your program end date (before the end of your grace period), and
- within **30 days of your OPT I-20 being issued** by OGI.

Your program end date is the last day of final exams of your graduating semester, and your I-20 will be shortened to that date. If you leave the U.S. during the 60-day grace period before filing, you lose eligibility. USCIS processing takes about 3–5 months and cannot be expedited, so plan ahead. Choose your requested start date carefully — it cannot be changed once review begins.

## How to apply (overview)
1. Submit the **Post-Completion OPT Request in the myGlobal portal** (UCID login → F-1 Practical Training), including the **$200 NJIT OPT record-management fee** receipt, your OPT quiz certificate, and a copy of your I-94. Allow about 7 business days.
2. OGI reviews and issues your **OPT-requested Form I-20** by email — review it carefully and report any error to OGI immediately.
3. File **Form I-765 with USCIS** (online or by mail — choose one), using the current form, fee, and address from the USCIS website. USCIS must receive it within 30 days of your I-20 issuance.

OGI cannot advise on the USCIS online system; direct USCIS questions to USCIS.

## STEM OPT (24-month extension)
If you earned a STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering, or Mathematics) degree, you may apply for a one-time 24-month STEM OPT extension of your post-completion OPT (a second extension is possible after a subsequent higher STEM degree).

Eligibility:
- Be currently on approved post-completion OPT and maintaining F-1 status.
- Have a degree from an accredited, SEVP-certified U.S. institution that is on the DHS STEM Designated Degree Program List (within the last 10 years). Not all NJIT degrees are STEM-eligible — your program's CIP code is on page 1 of your I-20; check with OGI if unsure.
- Be working (or have accepted paid employment) with a U.S. employer enrolled in **E-Verify**, directly related to your major, **full-time (more than 20 hours/week)**. No self-employment.

When to apply: USCIS must receive your application before your current OPT (or cap-gap) expires, no sooner than 90 days before your OPT end date, and within 60 days of the date OGI issued your STEM extension I-20. Filing on time **automatically extends your work authorization for 180 days** while the application is pending. USCIS processing takes about 3–5 months.

How to apply (overview): submit the STEM OPT Extension Request in the myGlobal portal with a completed **Form I-983** (signed by you and your employer), your diploma, transcript, current EAD, I-94, the Canvas STEM module certificate, and the **$200 NJIT STEM OPT record-management fee** receipt. OGI processes in about 5–7 business days and issues your STEM extension I-20; then file **Form I-765 with USCIS** using the current form, fee, and address from the USCIS website.

## Ph.D. students
Ph.D. students may apply for post-completion OPT before officially completing the program; OGI uses your dissertation defense date or your final-dissertation submission date as the program end date on the I-20. You may not hold a TA/RA or other on-campus job after your program end date.

## Confirm with OGI
Start every OPT or STEM application with OGI, and always verify the current USCIS form, fee, and address: https://www.njit.edu/global
