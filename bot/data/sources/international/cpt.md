# Curricular Practical Training (CPT)

## What it is
Curricular Practical Training (CPT) is work authorization for F-1 students to do a co-op or internship that is an integral part of your degree's established curriculum. CPT is authorized by the Office of Global Initiatives (OGI), with approval from Career Development Services (CDS) and your academic adviser; it is not guaranteed. You must register for a co-op course while on CPT. NJIT cannot be the CPT employer — work for NJIT on campus is "on-campus employment," which does not need CPT.

## Eligibility
- Be in valid F-1 status and physically present in the U.S.
- Have been enrolled full-time for at least one full academic year (a fall and a spring) before CPT; a break in enrollment can reset this.
- Be in good academic standing: cumulative GPA of at least 2.2 (undergraduate) or 3.0 (graduate).
- Stay enrolled full-time during CPT, and the job must be directly related to your current program.
- Location: in fall and spring the CPT site must be within about 3 hours' driving distance of NJIT (USCIS requires on-campus physical presence); summer CPT may be elsewhere unless it is your graduation semester.
- You must receive a new Form I-20 with CPT authorization BEFORE you start work — paid or unpaid.

## How to apply
Start early — approvals take time, and the process runs through Handshake:
1. Meet your academic and career advisers about co-op eligibility.
2. Complete the CPT course and quiz in Canvas and upload the CPT Course Badge.
3. Submit the Handshake application (needs approval from CDS, your faculty/academic adviser, the employer, and OGI).
4. Register for the co-op course for your program.
5. OGI reviews after the other approvals (processing up to about 7 business days) and issues a new I-20 with CPT authorization.
6. Begin only on the authorization start date on your new I-20, with final OGI approval. Off-campus CPT also requires a Social Security Number.

## Part-time vs. full-time
Part-time CPT is up to 20 hours per week; full-time CPT is more than 20 hours per week. There is no limit on part-time CPT, but **more than 364 days of full-time CPT cancels your OPT eligibility** — plan carefully.

## Ph.D. students
CPT for Ph.D. students is strictly limited to collecting data needed for dissertation research, must be justified by the advisor, and **requires Graduate Studies Office (GSO) approval**. CPT cannot extend beyond your dissertation defense or program completion.

## Important
CPT cannot be used to delay or extend your program; OGI will not extend an I-20 for late graduation caused by CPT. You may work only for the approved employer and only within the authorized dates.

CPT is authorized by OGI (with CDS and your academic adviser). Start the application through Handshake well in advance and confirm all details with OGI: https://www.njit.edu/global
