# GSA Travel Award Packet (NJIT Graduate Student Association)

The GSA Travel Award helps currently enrolled NJIT graduate students who are presenting
their research/work at off-campus conferences. The latest modification to this document was
made and approved on April 24th, 2025. For questions about eligibility, contact the GSA's
VP of Finance at gsa-vp-finance-group@njit.edu.

## What Students Must Submit

1. Students must submit a **Chrome River Pre-Approval Report** at least **2 weeks** prior to
   conference travel. This Pre-Approval Report must use your advisor's grant index (if
   requesting additional funding beyond the GSA Travel Award) or your department's
   index/allocation number. Your advisor's or department's approval is required for travel.
2. After the conference, students must submit the **GSA Post-Travel Google Form** within
   **30 days** of conference travel. On submission, you receive the GSA index/allocation
   number to use for your Chrome River Expense Report.
3. After submitting the Post-Travel Google Form, students must submit a **Chrome River
   Expense Report** using the GSA index number provided by the Google Form, also within
   **30 days** of travel. If receiving partial support from your advisor or department, use
   those allocation numbers in addition to the GSA allocation.

**Disclaimers:** Applying for a GSA Travel Award does NOT guarantee you will receive an
award; awards are contingent on available funding and on the applicant fulfilling the
requirements. The final decision is made after the Chrome River Expense Report with original
receipts is submitted, and all Travel Award decisions are final.

## I. Process Summary

**Pre-Travel Notification** — Timeline: at least 2 weeks prior to the actual conference date.
Form: Chrome River Pre-Approval Report using advisor/department index, completed at least 2
weeks prior to the actual conference date.

**Post-Travel** — Timeline: within 30 days of travel. Forms: GSA Post-Travel Google Form, and
an Expense Report submitted on the Chrome River Portal (completed within 30 days of travel).

## III. Rules and Regulations

1. Hereafter the term "conference" shall refer to reputable academic-related conferences or
   workshops.
2. Travel Awards will be granted only to currently enrolled graduate students presenting
   their research/work at an off-campus conference. There is no distinction between a poster
   presentation and an oral presentation.
3. Online conferences are eligible for the Travel Award. For online conferences, the Travel
   Award will only cover the registration and/or registration fees by default. All other
   expenses incurred for online conference presentations can only be reimbursed if justified
   by the student and approved by the advisor/department in the Chrome River Pre-Approval
   report.
4. For local conferences (within the Newark/New York City metropolitan area) where lodging is
   not required, only registration fees and travel expenses (car mileage, taxi, train, etc.)
   will be covered by the Travel Award.
5. For non-local conferences, the Travel Award can be used to fund: registration fees; travel
   costs (airfare, train/bus tickets, etc.); lodging (hotels, motels, etc. — AirBNBs, VRBOs,
   or other vacation rentals are NOT eligible for reimbursement); and meal expenses.
6. Meals may be reimbursed on a per-diem basis. For per-diem rates for each state, visit the
   U.S. General Administration Services.
7. A student is eligible for a total maximum of **$900**, inclusive of all Travel Awards per
   fiscal year. The fiscal year runs from July 1st through June 30th. The fiscal year an
   individual Travel Award is counted toward depends on the fiscal year in which the Chrome
   River Expense Report is submitted by the applicant; submissions must follow the deadlines
   in the Process Summary.
8. Applicants are required to get travel approved by their advisor or department admin through
   the Chrome River Pre-Approval Report, using the advisor or department's index/allocation
   number.
9. Funds will only be reimbursed after the travel has concluded, on the condition that the
   required information is submitted in the Chrome River Expense Report with all required
   documents and within the proper time frame.
10. Reimbursements are made on the basis of the actual expenses incurred during travel, not
    on the basis of estimated costs. All expenses submitted through the Chrome River Expense
    Report (except meal per-diems) must have matching original receipts (digital or scanned
    physical receipts), along with matching bank or credit card statements.
11. It is the student's responsibility to follow up with their advisors and department admin
    to ensure the Chrome River Pre-Approval is approved at least 2 weeks prior to conference
    travel, and to ensure the GSA Post-Travel Google Form and Chrome River Expense Report are
    submitted within 30 days of conference travel.
12. All award decisions will be made after the Chrome River Expense Report is submitted and
    reviewed; all award decisions made at this time will be final.

## IV. Application Process

**1. Chrome River Pre-Approval:** The applicant fills out the Chrome River Pre-Approval Report
and submits it at least two weeks prior to the conference date, using the advisor/department's
index/allocation number. Information/documents needed: a breakdown of predicted expenses for
travel; an abstract of the presentation or paper being presented; and the index/allocation
number for the advisor/department approving the travel.

**2. GSA Post-Travel Google Form:** After the conference travel is complete, the applicant
submits the GSA Post-Travel Google Form within 30 days of travel. It requires the same
documentation as the Chrome River Expense Report. On submission, the applicant receives the
GSA Travel Award Index Number to use for the Chrome River Expense Report.

**3. Chrome River Expense Report:** After submitting the Post-Travel Google Form, the applicant
submits a Chrome River Expense Report using the GSA allocation number provided by the form (do
not use the advisor/department's index/allocation number). Expenses must be itemized, with only
one receipt per expense item (e.g., each taxi or Uber ride as a separate item). Each student is
limited to a maximum of $900 per fiscal year.

### Required Documents for Chrome River Expense Report

- **Approved Chrome River Pre-Approval:** Pre-approval by the applicant's advisor or department
  must be submitted at least 2 weeks prior to the conference date. A completed Pre-Approval
  report must be attached to the Chrome River Expense Report, or the Travel Award application
  will not be approved.
- **Screenshot of Conference Website:** Must include the name of the conference and clearly
  indicate the dates and location of the conference.
- **Proof of Registration:** Email confirmation from the conference, or proof of
  payment/invoice for the registration fee.
- **Copy of Student's Poster/Presentation/Paper:** A copy of the work presented at the
  conference.
- **Proof of Attendance:** The conference session schedule with the student's name and
  presentation title, plus a picture of themselves at the conference or a photo of the
  conference badge.
- **Original Receipts** for all purchases, clearly indicating proof of payment and amounts.
- **Proof of Expense:** Bank or credit card statements matching the expenses submitted and
  original receipts. For cash payments, original receipts are sufficient.

### Additional NJIT Travel Policies

- Car rental fees will not be reimbursed through travel awards.
- If traveling by personal vehicle, report total traveled mileage in the mileage tile
  ("underground Transportation") in Chrome River. NJIT reimburses travel with a personal
  vehicle using the IRS Standard Mileage Rate for business usage, currently $0.70 per mile (as
  of 2/10/2025). Report traveled mileage based on the Google Maps itinerary for your trip. You
  can also submit your EZ-Pass receipt or physical receipts from tolls incurred during travel.
- Hotel accommodation costs must be reasonable, meaning the price is comparable to the average
  hotel costs in the area.
- All original receipts (for hotels, travel, etc.) must be presented in the Chrome River
  Expense Report.
- AirBNBs, VRBOs, and other similar vacation rental platforms are not considered hotel
  accommodations and will not be reimbursed.
- In the case of a failure to submit the required documents, or to follow the procedures
  listed above, no award will be provided.
- Students may be asked to submit the original receipts for evaluation. In case of major
  discrepancies, the GSA may disqualify your application.
- External funding does not disqualify students from securing a travel award, but is taken
  into consideration for the GSA's reimbursement. For students with external funding, GSA can
  cover the amount not funded by external funding. However, failure to disclose external
  funding may lead to disqualification.

## V. Award Selection Criteria

The following criteria are taken into account when approving GSA Travel Award applications:

1. Completeness of the Travel Award Application, following the requirements in the Travel Award
   Packet, including but not limited to: presenting your own work/research at a
   conference/workshop; travel approved by advisor or department at least 2 weeks prior to the
   conference date; all documents required for the Expense Report submitted correctly; NJIT's
   University-wide travel policies followed; and legitimacy of documents submitted. Submission
   of illegitimate documents will cause the application to be rejected and may preclude the
   applicant from receiving future Travel Awards.
2. The level, prestige, and scope of the conference (International/National/Regional;
   ACS/IEEE/AMIA/ASME or other professional organizations, etc.).
3. Additional external funds received that would cover presentation and/or travel expenses.
