# GSA Community Guidelines

These guidelines apply to all GSA Discord server channels and GSA-hosted events.

## 1. Respect and Professionalism
Treat all members with respect regardless of background, nationality, program, or academic status. Personal attacks, harassment, or discriminatory language will not be tolerated and may result in removal from the server.

## 2. Constructive Communication
Keep discussions constructive. Disagreements are welcome; personal insults are not. When raising concerns about academic or administrative matters, focus on the issue rather than individuals.

## 3. Relevance
Post content in the appropriate channels. Academic questions belong in #academic-help, event discussion in #events, and general conversation in #grad-lounge. Off-topic posts may be moved or removed.

## 4. Privacy
Do not share personally identifiable information about other students without their consent. This includes student ID numbers, grades, or personal contact details.

## 5. No Spam or Self-Promotion
Excessive self-promotion, advertising, or unsolicited promotional content is not allowed. Relevant opportunities (jobs, conferences, research positions) may be shared in designated channels.

## 6. Academic Integrity
Requests for academic dishonesty (sharing exam answers, writing papers for others, etc.) will result in immediate removal and may be reported to NJIT's Office of Academic Integrity.

## 7. Reporting Violations
Report violations to a GSA officer via DM or use the `/feedback` command to submit a confidential report. All reports are taken seriously and reviewed by GSA officers and, where appropriate, NJIT administration.

## 8. Enforcement
Violations may result in a warning, temporary mute, or permanent ban depending on severity. GSA officers have the final say in moderation decisions.

---
*These guidelines are subject to change. Last updated by the GSA Executive Board.*
