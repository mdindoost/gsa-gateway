# MMI Workshop Series — Knowledge Base

## Q: What is the MMI Workshop?
**A:** The MultiMedia Intelligence Workshop Series (MMI) is an annual research event hosted at NJIT (New Jersey Institute of Technology). It brings together students, faculty, and international researchers working at the intersection of multimedia, artificial intelligence, and multimodal learning. Each edition is organized around a focused theme and features invited talks from leading researchers worldwide. Topics include multimedia retrieval, multimodal reasoning, vision-language systems, trustworthy AI, information access, and human-centered computing. It is co-organized by the NJIT Computer Science Department and the Graduate Student Association (GSA).

## Q: Who organizes the MMI Workshop?
**A:** The MMI Workshop Series is organized by Mohammad Dindoost (Ph.D. Candidate and VP Academic Affairs at GSA NJIT), with Workshop Chair Prof. Vincent Oria (Professor and Chair of the Computer Science Department at NJIT), and Prof. Shin'ichi Satoh (National Institute of Informatics, Tokyo, Japan). Mohammad Dindoost can be reached at md724@njit.edu.

## Q: How many editions of MMI have been held?
**A:** Two editions have been held so far — MMI 2025 (June 27, 2025) and MMI 2026 (May 21–22, 2026), both at NJIT in Newark, NJ. Together they have had 13 invited speakers, 12 participating institutions, and 200+ attendees and registrations.

## Q: What was MMI 2026 about?
**A:** MMI 2026 was titled "Safe and Explainable MultiMedia Intelligence" and took place on May 21–22, 2026 at NJIT (GITC 1100, Newark, NJ). The theme focused on how to build multimedia AI systems that are not just powerful, but safe and explainable. Topics covered safety in agentic AI, bias in machine learning, interpretability, uncertainty quantification, augmented reality, and how interaction modality shapes risk in information-seeking systems. The event had 10 invited speakers, 142 registered attendees, and 12 participating institutions.

## Q: Can graduate students attend MMI?
**A:** Yes. MMI is open to all NJIT graduate students and researchers. Past editions have had 100+ registered attendees. The workshop is free to attend and is designed to give students direct access to leading international researchers and emerging research directions.

## Q: What topics does the MMI Workshop cover?
**A:** MMI covers research at the intersection of multimedia and AI, including: multimedia retrieval, multimodal reasoning, vision-language models, trustworthy AI, explainable AI, safe AI, information retrieval, computer vision, augmented reality, lifelogging, knowledge graphs, uncertainty quantification, and human-centered computing.

## Q: Who is Prof. Tat-Seng Chua?
**A:** Prof. Tat-Seng Chua is a Professor at the School of Computing, National University of Singapore (NUS) and a Distinguished Visiting Professor at Tsinghua University. He is co-Director of NExT (a joint research center between NUS and Tsinghua). He spoke at MMI 2026 on "Safety in the Agentic AI Era." His research focuses on multimodal foundation models, responsible AI, and conversational search. He is a Fellow of the Singapore National Academy of Science and a recipient of the 2015 ACM SIGMM Achievements Award.

## Q: Who is Prof. Vincent Oria?
**A:** Prof. Vincent Oria is a Professor and Chair of the Computer Science Department at NJIT. He has served as Workshop Chair for both MMI 2025 and MMI 2026. His research interests include multimedia databases, spatiotemporal databases, high-dimensional search, and recommender systems. He is a recipient of the 2015 ACM SIGMOD Test-of-Time Award.

## Q: Who is Mohammad Dindoost?
**A:** Mohammad Dindoost is a Ph.D. Candidate and Research Assistant in Computer Science at NJIT, and VP Academic Affairs for the GSA. He is the founder and series coordinator of the MMI Workshop. His research focuses on large-scale graph analytics and Graph Neural Networks (GNNs). He received the Outstanding Student Paper Award at IEEE HPEC 2025. He can be reached at md724@njit.edu.
