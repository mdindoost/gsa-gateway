## Q: What is GSA?
**A:** The Graduate Student Association (GSA) is the official governing body representing all graduate students at New Jersey Institute of Technology (NJIT). GSA advocates for graduate student interests, organizes academic and social events, manages funding for graduate organizations, and ensures student voices are heard in university decision-making. Membership is automatic for all enrolled graduate students — no sign-up required.

## Q: How many students does GSA represent?
**A:** GSA represents over 2,400 graduate students at NJIT across all programs and departments.

## Q: What is GSA's annual budget?
**A:** GSA manages an annual budget of approximately $150,000 in graduate student activity fees for the 2025–2026 academic year. This funds events, department representative activities, travel awards, and student programs.

## Q: Where is the GSA office?
**A:** The GSA office is located at Campus Center 110A. Office hours are weekdays from 11:00 AM to 5:00 PM. You can also reach the GSA President at gsa-pres@njit.edu or use `/contact president` for full officer contact info.

## Q: How do I contact GSA?
**A:** Email the President at gsa-pres@njit.edu, the VP Academic Affairs at gsa-vpa@njit.edu, or visit the office at Campus Center 110A on weekdays 11 AM–5 PM. You can also follow GSA on Instagram at @njit.gsa, or use `/contact` in this Discord to look up any officer directly.

## Q: What does the VP of Academic Affairs do?
**A:** The VP of Academic Affairs leads academic initiatives for graduate students, chairs the Three Minute Research Presentations (3MRP) competition, chairs Graduate Research Day, and represents graduate students on university academic committees. Contact: gsa-vpa@njit.edu.

## Q: How do I become a GSA officer?
**A:** GSA Executive Board elections are held annually. Applications are submitted to gsa@njit.edu. Candidates present at General Assembly meetings and all enrolled graduate students in good academic standing may apply and vote.

## Q: What is a Department Representative?
**A:** Department Representatives (DepReps) are elected graduate students who serve as liaisons between their academic department and the GSA General Assembly. They advocate for their department's students, attend GA meetings, and manage up to $1,000 in departmental engagement funding per year. There are 32 DepReps across 16 departments in 5 colleges.

## Q: How do I become a DepRep?
**A:** Submit an email expressing your interest in serving as a Department Representative to the GSA. The position is filled through departmental election or appointment. The GSA Executive Board reviews your application and forwards it to the Graduate Studies Office for eligibility verification. You must be enrolled, in good academic standing, and meet minimum GPA requirements.

## Q: What funding is available for DepReps?
**A:** Each department can receive up to $1,000 per academic year for engagement activities. To apply, submit a one-paragraph project proposal to gsa-pres@njit.edu, get departmental approval, and submit a post-project report after completion. Contact the GSA President or VP Finances for details.

## Q: What is 3MRP?
**A:** The Three Minute Research Presentations (3MRP) is GSA's annual competition where graduate students present their research in exactly 3 minutes using one slide. There are $3,000 in awards plus travel prizes, and an Audience Popular Vote award. It is organized by the VP of Academic Affairs each April. Apply and learn more at gsanjit.com/3mrp.

## Q: What is Graduate Research Day?
**A:** Graduate Research Day is GSA's annual research showcase held each November at the Campus Center Ballroom. Graduate students present digital posters of their research. There are $3,000 in travel awards for winners and lunch is provided. Contact gsa-vpa@njit.edu for information or to participate.

## Q: What is the MMI Workshop?
**A:** The NJIT Workshop on Multimedia Intelligence (MMI) is a research workshop organized by GSA that brings together researchers in multimedia and AI. The 2026 edition (MMI 2026) is scheduled for May 21–22, 2026. See gsanjit.com/mmi2026 for details and registration.

## Q: Is there a travel award for conferences?
**A:** Yes. GSA offers Travel Awards to support graduate students presenting their work at academic conferences. Award applications are available at the GSA office or through gsanjit.com. The 3MRP and Graduate Research Day competitions also include travel prizes for winners.

## Q: How do I apply for a travel award?
**A:** GSA Travel Award applications are available at the GSA office (Campus Center 110A) and on gsanjit.com. Submit your completed application to gsa-vpa@njit.edu. You can also find the direct form link in the funding section of `/resources funding`. Awards are granted to students who are presenting at recognized academic conferences.

## Q: What events does GSA organize?
**A:** GSA organizes Graduate Research Day (November), Three Minute Research Presentations / 3MRP (April), the MMI Workshop, weekly Friday Happy Hours at Highlander Pub, an AI Webinar Series, the Science Diplomacy Forum, International Student Meet & Greet events, and General Assembly meetings. Use `/events` to see what is coming up, or follow @njit.gsa on Instagram.

## Q: What is the GSA Friday Happy Hour?
**A:** GSA hosts a weekly Friday Happy Hour from 4–7 PM at the Highlander Pub on the 3rd floor of the Campus Center. It is open to all graduate students. Bar access requires a valid 21+ ID. Check Instagram @njit.gsa for any date changes or special events.

## Q: What is the General Assembly?
**A:** The General Assembly (GA) is the main legislative body of GSA, made up of all Department Representatives and the Executive Board. It meets approximately 7 times per semester to vote on policies, budgets, and funding. A quorum of 9 departments is required. Graduate students are welcome to attend and bring concerns.

## Q: Where can I find the GSA Constitution?
**A:** The GSA Constitution and Bylaws are publicly available at gsanjit.com/about-6 under Documents and Forms. A direct PDF download link is on that page.

## Q: How do I submit an initiative or idea to GSA?
**A:** Use the `/initiative` command right here in Discord. A form will appear asking for your initiative title, description, category, and whether you would like GSA to follow up with you. Your identity is kept anonymous unless you choose to share contact information. GSA officers review all submissions regularly.

## Q: What resources are available for international students?
**A:** The Office of Global Initiatives (OGI) supports international students with visa advising, orientation, global programming, and study abroad opportunities. GSA co-organizes the International Student Meet & Greet each semester. Use `/contact global_initiatives` for OGI contact info, or `/resources international` for a full list of resources.

## Q: Where can I get mental health support?
**A:** NJIT's Counseling Center (C-CAPS) offers free confidential counseling at Campus Center Room 260 (Mon–Fri 9 AM–5 PM). For after-hours crises, call 973-596-3414. The Center for Peer Wellness Coaching (behind NJIT Smashburger) offers free peer coaching with no appointment needed during open hours. Use `/contact counseling_center` or `/contact peer_wellness` for details.

## Q: How do I register for a GSA event?
**A:** Most GSA events are free and open to all enrolled graduate students. RSVP links are listed in the `/events` command. Some events require check-in through the Campus Community app. Walk-ins are welcome for most events. Follow @njit.gsa on Instagram for announcements.

## Q: How can I stay updated on GSA news and events?
**A:** Follow GSA on Instagram at @njit.gsa for real-time announcements and event photos. Use `/events` in this Discord for the full event calendar. Visit gsanjit.com for the official website and documents. GSA also sends periodic emails to all graduate students through the NJIT mailing list.

## Q: What academic resources does NJIT offer graduate students?
**A:** NJIT offers the Library (research databases, study spaces, Zotero workshops), Graduate Studies Office (academic policies, enrollment, thesis support), and subject matter advisors in each department. Use `/resources academic` for a full list with links.

## Q: Are there funding opportunities for graduate students?
**A:** Yes. Funding sources include: GSA Travel Awards, 3MRP competition prizes, Graduate Research Day travel awards, and DepRep department funding. NJIT also offers assistantships, fellowships, and financial aid. Use `/resources funding` for a full list, or contact gsa-vpa@njit.edu.

## Q: How can I contact a specific GSA officer or campus office?
**A:** Use the `/contact` command. Type `/contact` alone for the full directory, or `/contact <name>` for a specific person — for example `/contact president`, `/contact vp academic affairs`, or `/contact counseling center`.

## Q: What is GSA's role in university governance?
**A:** GSA representatives sit on the NJIT Board of Trustees (AARC and CLC committees), the University Senate, and Faculty Senate committees (CITLAR, CGE). The President serves as University Senator and the VP Finances serves on the Faculty Senate CGE. These roles give graduate students a formal voice in NJIT's academic and administrative decisions.

## Q: How can I get funding for a graduate student club or organization?
**A:** Recognized Graduate Organizations (RGOs) can apply for GSA funding. Review the Club Finance Bylaws at gsanjit.com/about-6 and submit your request through your Department Representative or directly to the GSA VP Finances at gsa-vpf@njit.edu.
