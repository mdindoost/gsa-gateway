# GSA Gateway — Frequently Asked Questions

This file is parsed automatically by the bot. Format is strict:
`## Q: <question>` followed by `**A:** <answer>`. Separate sections with a blank line.

---

## Q: What is the GSA?
**A:** The Graduate Student Association (GSA) at NJIT is the official representative body for all graduate students at the New Jersey Institute of Technology. GSA advocates for graduate student interests at the university level, organizes academic and social events, connects students with campus resources, and gives graduate students a formal voice in university governance. Membership is automatic for all enrolled graduate students.

## Q: Who are the current GSA officers?
**A:** The GSA Executive Board includes a President, VP Academic Affairs, VP Finance, and Secretary. Officer contact information is available via the `/contact` command. You can also find up-to-date officer information on the NJIT Highlander Hub page for GSA.

## Q: How do I become a GSA member?
**A:** Membership is automatic! Every student currently enrolled in a graduate program at NJIT is automatically a GSA member. There is no application, fee, or sign-up process. You can get involved by attending GSA events, joining a committee, or running for an officer position during elections each spring.

## Q: How do I submit a student initiative or idea to GSA?
**A:** Use the `/initiative` command right here in Discord! A form will pop up asking for your initiative's title, description, category, and whether you'd like GSA to follow up with you. Your identity is kept anonymous unless you choose to share contact information. GSA officers review all submissions regularly.

## Q: What academic resources does NJIT offer graduate students?
**A:** NJIT offers a range of academic support services for graduate students including: the NJIT Library (database access, research consultations, interlibrary loans), the Writing Center (free writing consultations), the Office of Graduate Studies (thesis/dissertation support), and individual department advisors. Use `/resources academic` to see a full list with links.

## Q: Are there funding opportunities for graduate students?
**A:** Yes! Graduate students at NJIT can access teaching and research assistantships, the NJIT Foundation Scholarships, external fellowships like the NSF GRFP, and federal student loans through the Financial Aid Office. GSA also occasionally administers small project grants. Use `/resources funding` for a full list, or contact the Graduate Financial Aid office via `/contact graduate_financial_aid`.

## Q: How do I register for a GSA event?
**A:** Most GSA events are free and open to all enrolled graduate students. RSVP links are listed in the `/events` command and on the NJIT Highlander Hub. Some events have limited capacity — RSVP early to secure your spot. Walk-ins are often welcome for larger events unless noted otherwise.

## Q: What is the grievance or complaint procedure for graduate students?
**A:** Graduate students with academic or administrative grievances should first speak with their faculty advisor or program director. If unresolved, contact the Office of Graduate Studies. For concerns about discrimination or harassment, NJIT's Office of Equity and Inclusion handles formal complaints. GSA can provide guidance on navigating these processes — reach out via `/contact vp_academic_affairs` or submit feedback anonymously with `/feedback`.

## Q: What resources are available for international graduate students?
**A:** NJIT's Office of Global Initiatives supports international students with F-1/J-1 visa advising, OPT/CPT processing, tax assistance (Sprintax), and cultural programming. GSA also holds an International Graduate Student Q&A session each semester. Use `/resources international` for a full list or `/contact international_student_services` for direct contact.

## Q: What mental health and wellness resources are available?
**A:** NJIT offers free confidential counseling through the Counseling Center (Campus Center, Room 260). Services include individual counseling, group therapy, crisis support, and wellness workshops. For after-hours emergencies, call 973-596-3414. The Campus Recreation Center is also a great resource for physical wellness. Use `/resources wellness` for more options.

## Q: Can I get funding to attend a conference or academic event?
**A:** Yes, several funding sources exist. Your department may have travel grant funds — ask your advisor first. The Office of Graduate Studies sometimes has supplemental travel funding. GSA is working to establish a direct student travel grant fund. The NSF GRFP and other external fellowships also often include conference travel allowances. Contact `/contact vp_academic_affairs` to discuss options.

## Q: How can I contact a specific GSA officer or campus office?
**A:** Use the `/contact` command! Type `/contact` alone to see the full directory, or `/contact <role>` for a specific person — for example `/contact VP Academic Affairs` or `/contact Counseling Center`. You can also submit anonymous feedback or questions using `/feedback`.

## Q: What is GSA's role in university academic policy?
**A:** GSA representatives sit on several NJIT faculty and administrative committees, including the University Senate. The VP Academic Affairs is specifically responsible for representing graduate students on matters of academic policy, curriculum, grading, and student rights. If you have concerns about academic policies affecting graduate students, contact the VP Academic Affairs via `/contact vp_academic_affairs`.

## Q: How can I stay updated on GSA news and events?
**A:** Follow GSA on NJIT's Highlander Hub platform, check this Discord server's announcements channel, and use the `/events` command to see upcoming events. The GSA Gateway website also lists current events and initiatives. GSA sends periodic email newsletters to all graduate students through the NJIT mailing list.
